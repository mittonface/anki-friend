**Keyword:** hood

### Primitive

In addition to the basic meaning of hood, this shape can be used for a glass canopy, such as that used to serve "pheasant under
glass." Note its difference from the element for wind: the second stroke is hooked inwards here. To help remember this detail,
think of the wind as blowing "out" and a glass canopy as keeping something "in." Among the related images suggested by this
primitive are: a monk's cowl, a riding hood, a helmet, and an automobile hood.
