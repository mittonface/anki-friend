**Keyword:** horns

### Primitive

This primitive element always appears at the top of the element to which it is related, and is always attached, or almost attached, to
the first horizontal line to come under it. The horns can never simply be left hanging in the air. When there is no line available, an
extra horizontal stroke (like a one) is added (for example, in "首".)
The meaning of this element is wide enough to embrace the horns of bulls, rams, billy goats, and moose, but not the family of
musical instruments. As with other elements with such "open" meanings, it is best to settle on one that you find most vivid and stick with that image consistently.
